<section class="landing-section">
    <h2>Why Secret Santa Needs Wishlists</h2>
    <p>Secret Santa is one of the most popular gift exchange traditions for workplaces, friend groups, and families. The concept is simple: everyone draws a name and buys a gift for that person. But the execution often falls flat. Without knowing what the other person wants, gift-givers are left guessing, and the result is frequently a gift that sits in a drawer unused.</p>
    <p>The most common complaints about Secret Santa exchanges are predictable: "I had no idea what to get them," "I got a candle again," or "They clearly just grabbed something last minute." These problems all stem from the same root cause: lack of information. When you do not know what someone wants, you default to safe, generic choices that rarely excite anyone.</p>
    <p>Wishlists solve this completely. When every participant in a Secret Santa exchange maintains a wishlist, the person assigned to buy their gift has a curated list of items that person actually wants. The gift-giver gets to choose from real options, the recipient gets something they love, and the entire exchange becomes genuinely enjoyable instead of obligatory.</p>
</section>

    <h2>How to Run a Secret Santa Exchange with ShareWants</h2>
    <p>Setting up a Secret Santa exchange with wishlists is straightforward. Here is a step-by-step approach that works for any group size:</p>
    <p><strong>Step 1: Everyone Downloads ShareWants.</strong> Have all participants download the free ShareWants app on their iPhone or Android device. Each person creates an account and sets up their profile.</p>
    <p><strong>Step 2: Build Your Wishlists.</strong> Give participants a deadline to add items to their wishlists. Encourage everyone to include at least 8 to 10 items at various price points so their Secret Santa has plenty of options within the budget. Items can come from any store, so people should feel free to add exactly what they want.</p>
    <p><strong>Step 3: Follow Each Other.</strong> Have all participants follow each other on ShareWants using profile links or QR codes. This way, once names are drawn, every gift-giver can immediately browse their assigned person's wishlist.</p>
    <p><strong>Step 4: Draw Names.</strong> Use your preferred method to assign Secret Santa matches. This can be done with traditional name drawing, an online randomizer, or by having the organizer assign pairs.</p>
    <p><strong>Step 5: Shop and Enjoy.</strong> Each Secret Santa opens ShareWants, finds their match's wishlist, and picks a gift they know will be appreciated. The exchange itself becomes the highlight it was always meant to be, with every person receiving something they actually wanted.</p>

<section class="landing-section">
    <h2>Tips for a Great Secret Santa Exchange</h2>
    <p>A few simple practices can make your Secret Santa exchange even more successful:</p>
    <p><strong>Set a clear budget.</strong> Agree on a spending limit upfront, and encourage participants to add items at or below that price point to their wishlists. This keeps things fair and takes the pressure off both the giver and the receiver.</p>
    <p><strong>Encourage variety.</strong> Ask participants to add items across different categories and price ranges. A wishlist with 10 diverse items gives the Secret Santa more flexibility than one with only 3 expensive options.</p>
    <p><strong>Set a wishlist deadline.</strong> Give everyone enough time to build their wishlists before names are drawn. A week is usually sufficient for people to browse their favorite stores and save items they genuinely want.</p>
    <p><strong>Keep it fun.</strong> The whole point of Secret Santa is to enjoy giving and receiving. With wishlists taking the stress out of gift selection, participants can focus on the fun parts: choosing a great gift, wrapping it creatively, and watching someone's face light up when they open it.</p>
</section>

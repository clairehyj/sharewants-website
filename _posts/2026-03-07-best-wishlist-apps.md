# 5 Best Wishlist Apps in 2026: Which One Is Right for You?

Finding the best wishlist app can feel like a gift-giving challenge in itself. There are dozens of options out there, each with different strengths, limitations, and target audiences. Some are built for holiday gift exchanges, some are locked to a single retailer, and some try to do everything at once.

To help you decide, we have compared five of the most popular wishlist apps available in 2026. We looked at each one from the perspective of a regular person who wants to create lists, share them with friends and family, and make the gift-giving process easier for everyone involved.

Here is what we found.

## 1. ShareWants

**Best for: People who want a social, cross-retailer wishlist experience**

ShareWants takes a different approach to wishlists by treating them as a social activity rather than a solitary shopping task. Instead of creating a list in isolation and sending a link to people once a year, you build your wishlist over time and let friends and family follow you to see what you want.

**Key features:**
- Add items from over 24 major retailers into a single list
- Follow friends and family to see their wishlists in a social feed
- Organize items into themed lists for different occasions
- Free to use with no premium tier required for core features
- Available on iOS and Android

**Pros:**
- The social feed model makes wishlists feel natural and low-pressure, not transactional
- Cross-retailer support means you are never locked into one store
- Clean, intuitive interface that does not overwhelm you with features
- Completely free

**Cons:**
- As a newer app, it has a smaller user base than long-established platforms
- No browser extension for adding items from desktop shopping

**Our take:** ShareWants is the strongest option if you value the social side of gift-giving. The ability to follow people and browse their lists casually, rather than waiting for someone to send you a link, changes how wishlists work in practice. It feels less like a transactional registry and more like a natural part of staying connected with the people you care about.

## 2. Amazon Wish Lists

**Best for: People who do most of their shopping on Amazon**

Amazon Wish Lists are the default choice for many people simply because Amazon is where they already shop. Creating a list is as easy as clicking "Add to List" on any product page, and sharing is straightforward.

**Key features:**
- Built directly into the Amazon shopping experience
- "Don't spoil my surprises" setting hides purchased items from the list owner
- Supports prioritization (highest, high, medium, low)
- Universal list feature allows adding non-Amazon items via browser extension

**Pros:**
- Seamless integration with the largest online retailer
- Familiar interface for anyone who already uses Amazon
- Purchase coordination prevents duplicate gifts
- Browser extension lets you add items from other websites

**Cons:**
- Heavily biased toward Amazon products; adding items from other stores is clunky
- The social experience is minimal, primarily designed for individual list-sharing
- Amazon's interface is cluttered and can feel overwhelming
- Your wishlist data contributes to Amazon's advertising and recommendation engine

**Our take:** If you buy almost everything from Amazon anyway, their built-in wish lists are convenient. But if you shop across multiple stores or want a more social experience, you will quickly feel the limitations. The universal list feature exists but is clearly an afterthought compared to the native Amazon integration.

---

## 3. Giftster

**Best for: Families who want to coordinate gift-giving for holidays**

Giftster has been around for years and has carved out a niche as a family-oriented gift list platform. It is designed specifically for coordinating gift exchanges within groups, making it a popular choice for families during the holiday season.

**Key features:**
- Create family groups where everyone can see each other's lists
- Mark items as "will give" to prevent duplicates
- Add items from any website
- Supports gift exchanges and Secret Santa organization

**Pros:**
- Purpose-built for family coordination, which it does well
- Group functionality makes holiday planning genuinely easier
- Cross-retailer item support
- Secret Santa and gift exchange tools are a nice bonus

**Cons:**
- The interface feels dated compared to modern apps
- Primarily useful during the holiday season; less relevant year-round
- Free tier has limitations; some features require a paid subscription
- The app experience is less polished than newer alternatives

**Our take:** Giftster is a solid choice if your primary use case is holiday gift coordination with a specific group of people, especially a large family. It does that job well. But it is less useful as an everyday wishlist tool, and the interface has not kept pace with modern design standards.

---

## 4. Elfster

**Best for: Organizing Secret Santa and gift exchanges**

Elfster started as a Secret Santa organizer and has expanded into a broader gift list platform. Its strength is still in facilitating gift exchanges, whether for families, friend groups, or workplace teams.

**Key features:**
- Secret Santa draw with customizable rules (exclusions, budget limits)
- Wish lists that integrate with the gift exchange
- Add items from any website or manually
- Messaging system for anonymous gift exchange communication

**Pros:**
- The best Secret Santa organization tool available
- Gift exchange features are thoughtful and well-executed
- Budget-setting helps keep exchanges fair
- Anonymous messaging adds fun to Secret Santa events

**Cons:**
- Heavily focused on exchanges rather than general wishlist use
- The app can feel bloated with features if you just want a simple list
- Ads in the free tier can be distracting
- Less useful outside of the holiday season or organized exchanges

**Our take:** If you are specifically organizing a Secret Santa or gift exchange, Elfster is hard to beat. The exchange mechanics, including exclusion rules, budget limits, and anonymous messaging, are genuinely well-designed. However, if you are looking for an everyday wishlist app that you will use year-round, Elfster is probably more tool than you need.

---

## 5. Google Keep

**Best for: People who want a simple, no-frills list they already have access to**

Google Keep is not technically a wishlist app. It is a note-taking tool. But plenty of people use it to maintain simple gift lists, and it is worth including because it is free, available everywhere, and requires no additional downloads if you are already in the Google ecosystem.

**Key features:**
- Simple checklists with optional labels and colors
- Share notes with specific Google accounts
- Pin important lists to the top of your notes
- Available across all devices with a Google account

**Pros:**
- Completely free with no ads
- If you already use Google services, there is nothing new to learn
- Dead-simple interface; no unnecessary features
- Syncs instantly across all your devices

**Cons:**
- No gift-specific features whatsoever: no purchase tracking, no duplicate prevention, no product details
- Shared lists show everything to everyone, including which items have been checked off
- No social features, no following, no feeds
- Adding product details (images, prices, links) requires manual effort

**Our take:** Google Keep is the duct-tape solution. It works if your needs are minimal and you just need somewhere to jot down a few ideas. But it lacks every feature that makes a dedicated wishlist app useful: product links, purchase coordination, price tracking, and social sharing. For anything beyond a quick personal note, you will want a purpose-built tool.

## How to Choose the Right Wishlist App

The best wishlist app for you depends on what you actually need. Here is a quick decision framework:

**Choose ShareWants if:** You want a modern, social wishlist experience that works across multiple retailers and stays useful year-round. You like the idea of following friends and family to see what they want, and you prefer a clean, free app without ads or clutter.

**Choose Amazon Wish Lists if:** You do most of your shopping on Amazon and want the simplest possible integration with your existing shopping habits. You do not mind being tied to one retailer for the majority of your list.

**Choose Giftster if:** Your main goal is coordinating holiday gifts within a family group. You need everyone in your family on the same platform, and the exchange features matter more than day-to-day wishlist use.

**Choose Elfster if:** You are organizing a Secret Santa or structured gift exchange and want purpose-built tools for drawing names, setting budgets, and communicating anonymously.

**Choose Google Keep if:** You want the absolute simplest option, do not need gift-specific features, and are already deeply embedded in the Google ecosystem.

## The Bottom Line

There is no single best wishlist app for everyone. The right choice depends on your habits, your needs, and who you are sharing lists with.

That said, if you are looking for a general-purpose wishlist app that combines cross-retailer flexibility with a genuinely social experience, [ShareWants](https://sharewants.com) is worth trying. It is free, it is designed for year-round use, and its social feed approach makes wishlists feel like a natural part of your relationships rather than a once-a-year chore.

Whatever you choose, the most important thing is to actually use it. A wishlist only works when it is up to date and shared with the right people. Pick a platform, add your items, and tell the people in your life where to find your list. Everyone will thank you for it.

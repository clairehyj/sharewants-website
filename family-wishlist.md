<section class="landing-section">
    <h2>Why Families Need a Shared Wishlist App</h2>
    <p>Gift-giving within families should be joyful, not stressful. But every birthday and holiday brings the same challenges: texting around to ask what people want, accidentally buying duplicates, and spending hours trying to find the right gift for a relative whose taste you are not quite sure about. A family wishlist app solves all of these problems by giving every family member a single place to share what they truly want.</p>
    <p>With ShareWants, each person in your family creates their own wishlist by saving items from any online store. Parents can add items from Target or Amazon, teenagers can save the latest sneakers from Nike, and grandparents can bookmark books or kitchen gadgets they have been eyeing. Everyone follows each other within the app, so when a birthday or holiday rolls around, you simply open ShareWants and browse the person's list. No awkward "what do you want?" conversations required.</p>
    <p>Unlike group text threads or shared spreadsheets, a dedicated family wishlist app keeps everything organized and up-to-date. Family members can add and remove items throughout the year, so their lists always reflect what they currently want. When it is time to shop, you see exactly what is on their mind, not what they mentioned six months ago.</p>
</section>

<section class="landing-section">
    <h2>How Families Use ShareWants Year-Round</h2>
    <p>The best part about a family wishlist is that it works for every occasion, not just Christmas. Here is how families make the most of ShareWants throughout the year:</p>
    <p><strong>Birthdays:</strong> Each family member keeps their wishlist updated so that when their birthday approaches, everyone already knows what to get. No last-minute scrambling or generic gift cards.</p>
    <p><strong>Holidays:</strong> Christmas, Hanukkah, and other gift-giving holidays become far less stressful when everyone has a visible wishlist. Parents can coordinate among relatives to cover different items, making sure the gifts are spread out and nothing is duplicated.</p>
    <p><strong>Back-to-School:</strong> Kids and college students can add school supplies, dorm essentials, or clothing to their wishlists. Grandparents and aunts who want to help can pick items right from the list.</p>
    <p><strong>Just Because:</strong> Sometimes a family member spots something they love but does not want to buy it themselves. Adding it to their ShareWants list means that when someone wants to surprise them, the perfect gift idea is already waiting.</p>
</section>
